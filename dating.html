I collected data on popular dating app user interfaces and analyzed the implicit biases behind their design.


I worked with Prof. Roderic Crooks from the UCI Department of Informatics to contribute to a paper on the interface of commonly used dating apps. During my tenure as an undergraduate researcher, I created profiles on twelve of the most commonly used dating apps in 2019 and collected data on the information requested of new users. I also performed preliminary market research on the online dating service market, and created schemas to code the collected data in an organized way.

The paper was published in the ACM CHI 2020 Virtual Proceedings as "Determining the Extractive Casting Mold of Intimate Platforms through Document Theory," which you can read here.
